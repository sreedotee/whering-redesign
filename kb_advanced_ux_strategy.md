# KB: Advanced UX & Product Strategy

## 1. User Segmentation: Regular vs. Power Users
A "UX-First" app must serve both without one "polluting" the other.
- **The Regular User (Discoverability):** Needs high **Signifiers**. (Icons with labels, clear buttons). They follow the "Standard Path."
- **The Power User (Efficiency):** Needs **Accelerators**. They use "Hidden" UI like long-presses, double-taps, or swipes to bypass standard menus.
- **The Solution:** **Progressive Disclosure.** Only show the 20% of features used 80% of the time on the surface. Tuck the "Power Tools" (detailed metadata, bulk editing) one level deeper or behind a long-press.

## 2. Feature Placement & Consolidation (Atomicity)
When two actions are "similar but separate," they create **Cognitive Friction.**
- **Pattern - Segmented Control:** If Actions A and B are just different "modes" of the same thing, use a toggle/segmented control at the top of the screen instead of two bottom tabs.
- **Pattern - Functional Cluster:** Group similar secondary actions into a **Floating Action Button (FAB)** that expands on tap. This keeps the nav bar focused purely on "Destination Hubs."

## 3. Social Proof & Trust Signals (The Feed)
In a fashion context, "Likes" are becoming commoditized/low-value. 
- **Low-Trust Signals:** Counts (Likes, Views, Shares). Good for "At a glance" but doesn't drive deep trust.
- **High-Trust (Vetted) Signals:** 
    - **UGC Proof:** "Worn by [X] real people." 
    - **Expert Vouching:** "Styled by a Verified Stylist."
    - **Sustainability Data:** "Verified Eco-Score" (Unique to Whering).
- **Placement:** Trust signals should be **Contextual.** (e.g., Don't show "Likes" in the Closet; show "Times Worn" vs "[X] People Saved this Outfit").

## 4. Finding the North Star
Whering is currently a "Wardrobe Database," which is a high-friction behavior.
- **The Shift:** Move from "Inventory Management" (Database) to "Usage Management" (Utility).
- **Proposed North Star Metric:** **"Weekly Outfits Worn"** (Utilization).
- **Impact on UX:** Every feature (Upload, Search, AI) must point toward the goal of the user *actually wearing* their clothes. If a feature doesn't contribute to "getting dressed," it may not deserve a primary tab.
